There are a few different ways of capturing blurred movement. First, you can use a long exposure technique, where you set your camera up on a tripod and dial in a slow shutter speed. If your tripod is sufficiently stable, you’ll end up with a shot that’s mostly sharp, but portrays the movement in terms of blur. Second, you can use a panning technique, where you follow the motion with your camera while using a moderately slow shutter speed (i.e., one that’s in the area of 1/20s to 1/60s). Third, you can use intentional camera movement to create the impression of movement. Here, you need to use a slow shutter speed (you can experiment with different values) while moving your camera in different directions.
